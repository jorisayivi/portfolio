Goal

Selected to represent our master’s program in the DRiM Game 2024 by Deloitte Risk Advisory, we worked on a real-world credit risk case involving corporate defaults across firm sizes and sectors, factoring in macroeconomic trends and GHG emissions.
The challenge had two phases: weeks of preparation followed by a two-day bootcamp with new datasets and time-constrained analysis at Deloitte's offices. We presented our results to a jury and won first place.

Data Exploration

Using SAS Viya, we analyzed default rates by sector and firm size across pre-COVID, COVID, and post-COVID periods. Defaults dropped sharply during COVID (due to state support), then rebounded unevenly. Micro-enterprises showed unexpected resilience, while SMEs proved more vulnerable. These insights guided our next steps.

Clustering

We built 70 sector–size time series and applied temporal K-means, revealing 3 groups: (A) stable, (B) moderately volatile, (C) highly unstable. This helped categorize risk profiles by trajectory.

Pattern Analysis

We identified sector patterns based on how default behavior varied across sizes. Some sectors (e.g., healthcare, insurance) were uniformly stable, while others showed internal asymmetries. This dual analysis improved interpretability.

Modeling

We used univariate AR-X models by size bracket to capture default dynamics and sensitivity to macroeconomic variables:

$$ \text{DefaultRate}_t = \alpha + \beta_1 \cdot \text{DefaultRate}_{t-1} + \beta_2 \cdot \text{DefaultRate}_{t-2} + \gamma_1 \cdot X_t + \gamma_2 \cdot X_{t-1} + \varepsilon_t $$

  • SMEs: High persistence, sensitive to inflation and energy prices.
  • Large firms: Less autoregressive, responsive to oil prices.
  • Mid-sized firms: Delayed response to energy shocks.
Model Limits
  • Short time series: Lower statistical power.
  • Limited timeline: No long-term cycles.
  • Few exogenous variables: Misses factors like credit conditions.
  • No firm-level granularity: Can't capture internal heterogeneity.

Integration of Energy Price Data

A final dataset (electricity, steam, oil prices) was added shortly before the presentation. These variables were statistically insignificant, likely due to lag effects and short coverage.

We recommended a correlation-tracking framework to monitor energy-default links by sector, relevant for future risk monitoring under transition scenarios.

Final Presentation & Outcome

We presented to professionals from Deloitte, SAS, and BNP. Our team placed 1st out of 7 universities, recognized for the technical strength and strategic clarity of our approach.
